# Weekly newsletter — Claude prompt template (Family Faith)

Use this every week (e.g. Monday). Paste into Claude and fill the bracketed parts. Then paste the output into beehiiv and schedule.

---

**Copy from here (including the prompt) — paste into Claude:**

---

You write a weekly newsletter called "Family Faith." It goes to parents and families who want ready-to-use Bible lessons and devotionals — no prep.

**This week’s inputs:**
- **Passage:** [paste the Bible reference and 1–2 sentence context]
- **Main point (one sentence):** [paste]
- **Discussion questions (3–4):** [paste the questions]
- **Verse of the week:** [reference + full verse]
- **Family take-home (one short activity):** [paste the at-home idea]

**Format requirements:**
1. Start with a 1–2 sentence intro that tees up the lesson.
2. **Lesson of the week** — Subhead, then: passage, main point, discussion questions. Keep it scannable (bullets or short paragraphs).
3. **Verse + question for the week** — Subhead, then the verse in quotes, then one question to ask at the table or bedtime.
4. **Family take-home** — Subhead, then one short “Do it once this week” activity (2–3 sentences).
5. End with a one-line sign-off and “[Your name] / Family Faith.”
6. Total length: ~400–600 words. Tone: warm, clear, no jargon. Assume busy parents.

**Output:** Write the full newsletter body only (no subject line unless I ask). Use clear section headings. Use the exact passage, verse, questions, and take-home I provided.

---

**After Claude responds:** Copy the body into beehiiv. Add your subject line (e.g. “One verse, one question — this week” or “Issue #X: [main point]”). Schedule for your send day.

**Where to get content:** Pull from the `curriculum/` folder — lessons, scope-and-sequence, devotionals. Or pick a passage and ask Claude to generate the main point, questions, and take-home first, then run this prompt with that output.
