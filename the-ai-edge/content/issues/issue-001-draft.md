## Tool of the Week: Perplexity

If you’re still opening 10 tabs to compare answers, stop. **Perplexity** is a search engine that uses AI to read the web and give you one clear answer — with sources.

**Why it’s worth it:** You ask in plain English (“What’s the best way to migrate from X to Y?” or “Summarize the key points of [article]”). You get a short, cited answer instead of a list of links. Huge time-saver for research, competitive checks, and “how do I…?” questions.

**How to use it:** Go to [perplexity.ai](https://www.perplexity.ai). Type your question. Use **Pro** (paid) for longer context and better reasoning, or stick with the free tier to start. Try it next time you’d normally Google and click through 5 articles — you’ll feel the difference.

## Quick Hits

- **Claude on Claude** — Anthropic’s own tips and prompt library. Free. Use it to level up how you prompt. [claude.ai/docs](https://claude.ai)
- **Notion AI** — Summarize pages, fix tone, and generate tables inside Notion. If your team already lives in Notion, this is the lowest-friction way to add AI. [notion.so/product/ai](https://www.notion.so/product/ai)
- **Canva AI** — Text-to-design, background removal, and instant layouts. Best for non-designers who need good-looking slides, social graphics, or one-pagers fast. [canva.com](https://www.canva.com)
- **Cursor** — Code editor with AI baked in (completion, chat, refactors). For anyone who writes or edits code, it’s the fastest path from “idea” to “working thing.” [cursor.com](https://cursor.com)
