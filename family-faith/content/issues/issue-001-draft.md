## Lesson of the week: Love from God (1 John 4:7–12)

This week we’re in 1 John 4. The big idea: **love doesn’t start with us — it starts with God.** He loved us first; we love others in response.

**Passage:** 1 John 4:7–12 (read it together — 2 minutes).

**Main point:** God is love. He showed His love by sending Jesus. When we know His love, we can love one another.

**Discussion questions (use at the table or in class):**
1. Where does love come from? (From God.)
2. How did God show His love to us? (He sent Jesus.)
3. Who is someone in our family we can love better this week? What’s one way we can show it?

**Activity:** Each person names one way they’ll show love to someone in the family this week. At the end of the week, ask: Did we do it? How did it go?

---

## Verse + question for the week

**Verse:** “Beloved, let us love one another, for love is from God.” — 1 John 4:7 (ESV)

**Question:** Who showed you love today? How can we show love to someone in our family this week?

Say the verse together once. Ask the question. Let everyone answer.

---

## Family take-home

**Do it once this week:** Pick one meal or bedtime. Read 1 John 4:7 and 4:11. Ask: “What’s one way we showed love today?” Then pray one sentence: “Thank you, God, for loving us. Help us love each other.”
