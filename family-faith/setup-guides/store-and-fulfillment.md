# Physical store + third-party fulfillment

Sell things you design (e.g. verse art, family faith tees, mugs) with **no inventory**. You upload the design; when someone buys, a third party prints and ships it. You never touch the product.

---

## How it works

1. **You design** — Art, quote, verse, or graphic. You own the file.
2. **You list** the product on a store (Etsy, Shopify, or Printful’s free store) and connect it to a **print-on-demand (POD)** partner.
3. **Customer buys** — They pay you (via the store).
4. **POD partner fulfills** — They print the design on the product (shirt, mug, poster, etc.) and ship it to the customer. You never pack or ship.
5. **You keep the difference** — Sale price minus product + print cost and fees.

---

## Recommended setup

### Option A: Printful + free Printful store (easiest start)

- **Printful** (printful.com) — POD partner. You upload designs; they print on 100+ products (tees, hoodies, mugs, posters, kids’ apparel, etc.) and ship.
- **Printful’s free storefront** — They host a simple store for you at yourname.printful.com. No monthly fee. You set prices; Printful’s cost is deducted when an order comes in; you get the rest.
- **Pros:** Free, no inventory, one dashboard.  
- **Cons:** Store is basic; less control over branding than your own site.

**Steps:** Sign up at Printful → Add products (choose product type, upload your design, set your price) → Enable “Store” and customize the page → Share the link in your newsletter footer and in issues.

---

### Option B: Printful or Printify + Etsy (good for traffic)

- **Printful** or **Printify** (printify.com) — POD partner. Printify has more suppliers and sometimes lower costs; Printful is simpler to start.
- **Etsy** — You list “digital” or “physical” products. For physical, you connect Etsy to Printful/Printify; when an Etsy order comes in, the POD partner gets the order and ships. Etsy brings its own audience (people search “faith t-shirt,” “verse art,” etc.).
- **Pros:** Etsy traffic, trusted marketplace, still no inventory.  
- **Cons:** Etsy fees (listing + transaction); you follow Etsy’s rules.

**Steps:** Create Etsy shop → Connect Printful or Printify (each has an Etsy integration) → Create products in POD dashboard (design + product type) → Publish to Etsy. Link your Etsy shop from your newsletter and landing page.

---

### Option C: Printful or Printify + Shopify (your own brand)

- **Shopify** (shopify.com) — Your own store (e.g. shop.familyfaith.co). You control look, copy, and domain.
- **Printful or Printify** — Connect via app; orders from Shopify auto-send to POD; they fulfill and (optionally) push tracking back to Shopify.
- **Pros:** Full control, professional store, can add digital products later (e.g. curriculum) on the same site.  
- **Cons:** Shopify monthly fee (~$29+); more setup.

**Steps:** Sign up for Shopify → Install Printful or Printify app → Create products (upload design, set price) → Add store link to newsletter and landing page.

---

## What to sell (Family Faith angle)

- **Apparel:** Tees and hoodies with a verse, “Family Faith,” or a simple faith graphic. Kids’ tees too.
- **Home / wall:** Verse art or family faith quote as a poster, canvas, or framed print.
- **Drinkware:** Mugs or tumblers with a verse or your logo/tagline.
- **Accessories:** Tote bags, stickers (often done via Printful or a sticker-specific POD).

Design in Canva, Figma, or any image editor. Export PNG or high-res PDF; upload to Printful/Printify per product.

---

## Linking the store to your business

- **Newsletter footer** — Add a line: “Shop our designs: [store link].”
- **Welcome email 3** — Optionally add a fourth “product”: “See our [tees / verse art] in the shop: [link].”
- **Landing page** — Add a “Shop” link next to (or under) the signup form.
- **Occasional issue** — Once a month or when you drop a new design: “New in the shop: [product]. [link].”

## Checklist (Option A — fastest)

- [ ] Sign up at Printful (or Printify).
- [ ] Create 1–3 products: upload design, choose product type, set your price (their base cost + your margin).
- [ ] Enable Printful store (or connect Etsy/Shopify).
- [ ] Add store URL to newsletter footer and landing page.
- [ ] Order one sample so you see quality before you promote.

---

## Costs (rough)

- **Printful/Printify:** No monthly fee. You pay only when an order is placed (their product + print cost; you set retail price).
- **Etsy:** Listing fees (small per listing) + transaction fee when you make a sale.
- **Shopify:** Monthly subscription (~$29+); plus transaction fee unless you use Shopify Payments.

Starting with **Printful + free Printful store** keeps monthly cost at $0 until you make sales.
