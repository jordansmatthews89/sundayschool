# Jesus Calms the Storm

**Leader guide**

---

## Basics

- **Title:** Jesus Calms the Storm
- **Scripture:** Mark 4:35–41
- **Memory verse:** “Who then is this, that even the wind and the sea obey him?” — Mark 4:41 (ESV)
- **Main point:** Jesus has authority over creation and over our fears. We can trust Him when life feels out of control.
- **Series:** Jesus: Life & Teachings (or standalone)
- **Time:** 45 min
- **Age group:** Families (parents + kids together); adapt as below for kids-only, youth, or adults.

---

## Before you start

- [ ] Read Mark 4:35–41.
- [ ] Bibles for everyone.
- [ ] Optional for kids: Blue cloth or paper for “storm” and “calm”; simple boat drawing.

---

## Lesson flow

### 1. Welcome / hook (5 min)

- **Question:** “Describe a time you were really scared. What helped?”
- **Families:** Kids answer first (“Have you ever been scared? What helped?”). Then parents: “When does life feel like a storm for you?”
- **Kids:** “Have you ever been in a storm or seen one? How did it feel?”
- **Youth/Adults:** “When does life feel like a storm — out of control?”

---

### 2. Read the Word (8 min)

- Read **Mark 4:35–41** together (leader reads, or take parts: narrator, Jesus, disciples).
- **Kids:** Act it out or use simple props (boat, “waves,” Jesus standing and speaking). Repeat verse 41 together.

---

### 3. Teach / explain (8 min)

- **Context:** Jesus had been teaching by the sea. He and the disciples get in a boat to cross to the other side. A fierce storm comes; the boat is filling with water. Jesus is asleep. The disciples wake Him and ask, “Do you not care?”
- **Main idea:** Jesus isn’t afraid. He speaks to the wind and sea — and they obey. The disciples are amazed: “Who is this?” He’s not just a teacher; He has power over creation. So when *our* life feels like a storm, we can go to Him. He cares, and He can calm what we can’t.
- **Key phrase:** “Peace! Be still!” (v. 39). Jesus is in charge.

---

### 4. Discuss (10 min)

1. Why do you think the disciples woke Jesus? What were they feeling?
2. What does Jesus’ calming the storm show about who He is?
3. When have you felt like the disciples — like God wasn’t paying attention?
4. **Application:** What’s one “storm” in your life right now? How can we trust Jesus with it this week?

---

### 5. Activity (8 min)

- **Families:** Each person names one thing that sometimes makes them afraid or worried. Parent prays a one-sentence prayer for the family: “Jesus, be with us in our storms.” Send home `family-take-home.md` so they can do “Pray when we’re scared” during the week.
- **Kids:** Draw two pictures: “My storm” (something that scares me) and “Jesus is with me” (Jesus in the boat). Say: “Jesus is with you in the storm.”
- **Youth:** In small groups: “What’s one thing we can pray about that feels out of control?” Pray for each other.
- **Adults:** Pair share: “What storm are you facing? How can we pray for you?” Close with brief prayer.

---

### 6. Wrap-up (5 min)

- **Restate:** Jesus has authority over the storm and over our lives. We can trust Him when we’re afraid.
- **Prayer:** Thank Jesus that He is with us in the storm; ask Him to help us trust Him and bring our fears to Him.

---

## For families

- **In class:** Use the “Families” prompts above so parents and kids can answer and pray together.
- **At home:** Print or email `family-take-home.md` so families can reread the passage, discuss, and try “Pray when we’re scared” during the week.
