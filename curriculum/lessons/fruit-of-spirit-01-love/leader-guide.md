# Fruit of the Spirit — Week 1: Love

**Leader guide**

---

## Basics

- **Title:** Love — The first fruit
- **Scripture:** Galatians 5:22–23; 1 John 4:7–12
- **Memory verse:** “Beloved, let us love one another, for love is from God.” — 1 John 4:7 (ESV)
- **Main point:** God is love, and He loves us first. When we know His love, we can love others.
- **Series:** Fruit of the Spirit, Week 1
- **Time:** 45 min
- **Age group:** Families (parents + kids together); adapt as noted for kids-only, youth, or adults.

---

## Before you start

- [ ] Read Galatians 5:22–23 and 1 John 4:7–12.
- [ ] Bibles for everyone (or one per pair).
- [ ] Optional: Paper and markers for kids “love in action” drawing.

---

## Lesson flow

### 1. Welcome / hook (5 min)

- **Question:** “When someone says ‘I love you,’ what do they mean?” (Different kinds: family, friends, romance, God.)
- **Families:** Let kids answer first (“Who loves you? How do you know?”), then parents add: “How do we show love in our family?”
- **Kids:** “Who loves you? How do you know?”
- **Youth/Adults:** “What’s the difference between liking someone and loving them the way the Bible talks about?”

---

### 2. Read the Word (8 min)

- Read **Galatians 5:22–23** together. Say: “The first fruit of the Spirit is love. Today we’ll see where that love comes from.”
- Read **1 John 4:7–12** (leader or round-robin).
- **Kids:** Read 1 John 4:7 and 4:11 only; repeat the memory verse together twice.

---

### 3. Teach / explain (8 min)

- **Context:** John is writing to believers. He says love isn’t something we invent — it comes *from God*. God showed His love by sending Jesus so we could have life and forgiveness.
- **Main idea:** We don’t love to earn God’s love. We love *because* He first loved us (1 John 4:19). So “love one another” isn’t just a rule — it’s a response to being loved by God.
- **Key phrase:** “Love is from God” (v. 7). When we love others, we’re showing that we know God.

---

### 4. Discuss (10 min)

1. Why does John say “whoever loves has been born of God” (v. 7)? What does that mean for us?
2. How did God show His love in this passage? (Sent His Son; Jesus gave His life.)
3. Who is someone in your life that’s easy to love? Who’s harder? What would it look like to love that person “from God” this week?
4. **Application:** What’s one specific way you can show love to someone in the next few days?

---

### 5. Activity (8 min)

- **Families:** Each person (parent and kid) draws or says one way they’ll show love to someone in the family this week. Share with each other. Send home `family-take-home.md` so they can do the “Do it together” activity during the week.
- **Kids:** Draw or write one way you’ll show love this week (e.g. share, say kind words, help at home). Share with the group.
- **Youth:** In pairs: “Tell about a time someone loved you when it was hard. How did it point you to God?”
- **Adults:** Pair share: “Name one person you’ll intentionally love this week and one concrete action.”

---

### 6. Wrap-up (5 min)

- **Restate:** The first fruit of the Spirit is love. It comes from God. He loved us first; we love others in response.
- **Prayer:** Thank God for His love in Christ; ask Him to help us love one another this week.

---
